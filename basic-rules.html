Basic Rules

Magic is a duel between two mages. You win by reducing your opponent to 0 life (most games), or by meeting a card’s special win condition.

What you need to know before your first game

  • Cards cost mana. Mana usually comes from lands.
  • You get one land play per turn. Lands don’t cost mana to play.
  • Creatures attack; creatures block. Most spells happen on your turn, but instants can happen on any turn.
  • “Tap” means rotate the card. Tapping is shown with the tap symbol .
  • Read the type line. It tells you what the card is (Creature, Instant, Land, etc.).

Setup

  • Each player shuffles their deck and draws 7 cards.
  • Decide who goes first. (Often: roll a die.)
  • Mulligan: If your hand is bad, you may reshuffle and draw 7 again, then put 1 card on the bottom for each mulligan taken.

Anatomy of a turn

Every turn has the same skeleton. Don’t panic — you’ll learn it by doing.

  1. Untap — Untap your tapped cards.
  2. Upkeep — “At the beginning of your upkeep” abilities happen.
  3. Draw — Draw a card. (The starting player usually skips their first draw.)
  4. Main phase — Play a land (if you haven’t), cast spells, activate abilities.
  5. Combat — Declare attackers, then blockers, then damage happens.
  6. Main phase (again) — Cast more spells after combat.
  7. End step / Cleanup — “At end step” abilities happen; damage wears off; discard down to hand size (usually 7).

Mana and casting spells

  • To cast a spell, you pay its mana cost (top-right of the card).
  • You usually pay by tapping lands for mana. Lands stay tapped until your next untap step.
  • Unused mana empties as steps/phases end. (No saving it “for later” unless a card says you can.)

Card types (the quick mental model)

  • Lands — Your main mana source. Played only on your turn, one per turn.
  • Creatures — Stay on the battlefield. They attack/block. Power/Toughness is bottom-right (e.g., 3/2).
  • Instants — Fast spells. Cast them any time you have priority (often in response).
  • Sorceries — Slower spells. Cast only during your main phase when the stack is empty.
  • Enchantments / Artifacts — Permanents that stay in play and provide ongoing effects.
  • Planeswalkers — Powerful permanents with loyalty abilities (use one per turn, on your turn).

Combat (how the punching works)

  • You choose which creatures attack and who they attack (the opponent, or sometimes planeswalkers).
  • Opponent chooses blockers.
  • Unblocked creatures deal damage to the opponent.
  • Blocked creatures deal damage to the creatures blocking / being blocked.
  • Most creatures have summoning sickness: a creature can’t attack or use tap abilities the turn it enters play unless it has haste.

Stack and timing (the one concept that makes everything click)

When a spell is cast or an ability is activated, it goes on the stack. Players can respond. The stack resolves last-in, first-out.

  • Instants (and abilities) can be used to respond to other spells.
  • If nobody responds, the top thing resolves. Then players get another chance to respond.
  • Most of the time, just remember: you can respond to what your opponent just did.

Keywords you’ll see a lot

Your fastest path to confidence is recognizing keywords. The Keyword Icons page is your cheat sheet.

  • Flying — blocked only by flying/reach.
  • Trample — extra damage spills over to the opponent.
  • First strike / Double strike — deals damage earlier (or twice).
  • Deathtouch — any damage is lethal to creatures.
  • Hexproof / Shroud — harder (or impossible) to target.

One last “save your brain” note

You do not need to memorize every rule. If you know the turn structure, mana, and combat, you can play. The rest is just learning what your cards do — which is the fun part.
