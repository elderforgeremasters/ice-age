Attunement Codex

The following is the complete Confluence Alignment Codex or in short, Attunement Codex. It is The Emberhold’s “Open Weave” doctrine made concrete: Powerful mages and planewalkers don’t anchor their magic abilities in certain mana colors — instead they read both temporal and spatial mana flows and assemble the optimal spell-kit for any given environment.

Attunement and color selection

The Ice Age isn’t just “cold weather.” It’s a mana crisis: the world is locked in winter, magic is dwindling, and the Weave (mana flow) has become erratic—some days a color is abundant, other days it’s thin, and sometimes it collapses into Null ({C}). This instability is why “show up with a fixed plan” can be dangerous: you might be trying to cast from a mana current that simply isn’t there. Therefore, before each duel, each non-role player performs Attunement: roll 6 mana color dice with faces . Here signifies a Null.

Stable Attunement vs Null Interference

Attunement resultDeck add-on
0 Null (stable)Add the Confluence Prism Pack (5 multicolor cards).
1–3 NullAdd up to that many Relic Toolkits (5 colorless cards). Maximum number of toolkits in a deck is 3.

Stability Count locks before Resolvers: count how many you rolled in your initial Attunement. That count determines Prism Pack vs Relic Toolkits (max 3). Resolvers may instruct you to reroll a for color selection, but that reroll does not change your add-on.
