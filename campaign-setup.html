ElderForge Campaign

The continent of Terisiare lies frozen. Magic dwindles. Alliances fracture. And beneath the Ohran Ridge, in a seam of stubborn warmth, a remote refuge endures: The Emberhold.

Officially, the Emberhold is neutral ground — a treaty-sanctioned sanctuary where apprentice mages can rest, trade, and train without becoming property of the great color academies. In an Ice Age, a safe hearth is not a luxury; it is a political miracle.

Unofficially, it is something sharper: a hidden research cell studying the world’s strangest threat — Null interference. When the mana flow stutters, anchored spellcraft becomes brittle. The Emberhold teaches Open Weave: the discipline of staying unanchored, reading the day’s mana like weather, and surviving instability through adaptation.

Every apprentice who arrives has already learned one harsh lesson simply by living through the Ice Age: cold is not scenery, it is a law of nature. That shared upbringing leaves a mark on the spark itself — a baseline Snowcraft that lets any Emberhold-trained mage call on a little winter mana from day one.

Beyond the Emberhold’s wards lies peril and opportunity. Rumors whisper of something ancient entombed beneath the vast Ronom Glacier — a draconic presence sealed before memory, stirring as the frost begins to crack. Some believe it will end the winter. Others believe it will end everything.

You are an apprentice mage, newly awakened to your spark. You will duel rivals, raid towers, descend into moonlit dungeons, and chase echoes through the ice. With every victory, your power ascends. Climb from apprentice to planeswalker — and remember: in the crucible of frost and ambition, only one may forge their name in the ice.

What ElderForge is

ElderForge is a campaign mode played as a series of Magic duels. Instead of building one fixed deck, you assemble a duel library each match from packs, toolkits, and Mythic Slots. The world is explored by drawing and resolving tiles from two bags: a dangerous Journey bag and a safe Sanctuary bag.

Core conventions

Basic lands live outside libraries

Basic lands are not shuffled into duel libraries. Keep basic lands in face-up piles. At any draw step, you may draw a basic land from outside the game instead of drawing from your library.

Zones that matter for campaign rewards

After a duel, only cards that were in play or in a graveyard count as eligible for capture rewards. Cards in exile and libraries are not eligible.

Card piles used during play

For gameplay, you keep a single draw pile for each color (White, Blue, Black, Red, Green), plus one Multicolor pile and one Colorless pile. (Rarity symbols still exist on the cards, but you do not sort piles by rarity.)

Loaner deck construction

Before each duel, you build a loaner library from the piles based on your Attunement result. This loaner library is not “owned”; it is returned to the piles after the duel.

  • Mono or two colors: draw 20 cards from the relevant color piles.
  • Three colors: draw 24 cards from the relevant color piles.
  • Five colors (equal): draw 25 cards (5 from each color).
  • Five colors (primary): draw 28 cards as 8/5/5/5/5 (8 of the primary color, 5 of each other color).

Null interference

After drawing your color cards, add extra cards based on Null results:

  • For each Null rolled, add one Relic Toolkit (5 cards) from the Colorless pile. There is no limit.
  • If you rolled 0 Null (stable Attunement), add the Confluence Prism Pack (5 cards) from the Multicolor pile instead of any toolkits.

Personal card slots by level

Starting at Level 1, you gain personal slots. These slots are filled only at Campfires during upgrades. Before a duel, you may add slotted cards to your loaner library (they are “yours” and do not return to the piles).

LevelSlots per sectionSections
Level 00—
Level 1–21W / U / B / R / G / Colorless / Multicolor
Level 3–42W / U / B / R / G / Colorless / Multicolor
Level 5–E3W / U / B / R / G / Colorless / Multicolor

Slot legality: Monocolor slots may only hold monocolor cards of that color. Multicolor cards may not populate color slots. Colorless slots hold only colorless cards. Multicolor slots hold only multicolor cards.

Fast cleanup: Because slot legality follows the card’s frame color, post-duel cleanup is quick: return all loaner cards to their piles, and re-stack your personal cards by section.

Snowcraft (Snowbank)

Snow-Covered basic lands are kept outside all libraries in five Snowbank piles (one per color).

Snowcraft: For each color you are playing this duel, you begin with Snowcraft 1 for that color. Each time you would draw a card, you may instead take a Snow-Covered basic of a color you are playing from its Snowbank pile. Doing so spends 1 Snowcraft for that color. When a color’s Snowcraft is 0, you cannot take additional Snow-Covered basics of that color this duel.

Rewards can permanently increase your Snowcraft for a specific color (maximum 3).

Loss Tokens

Loss tokens are persistent penalties you can accumulate after losing duels. They remain until healed at a Town or Campfire.

TokenEffectFloor
Shockmark-1 starting lifeStarting life cannot be reduced below 5.
Stormlash-1 starting hand sizeStarting hand size cannot be reduced below 3.

Gaining loss tokens: On a duel loss, gain 1 Ascension and return the failed tile to its bag. Then take exactly one loss token based on the source of the final damage that killed you: Shockmark if a creature dealt the killing blow, or Stormlash if a noncreature source dealt the killing blow.

Healing loss tokens:

  • Town — Sanctum: Remove all loss tokens of a single kind (remove all Shockmarks or remove all Stormlashes).
  • Campfire — Rest: Remove all loss tokens of a single kind (remove all Shockmarks or remove all Stormlashes).

Duel rewards

Card capture rule

After each win, the winner may choose one card the opponent owns that was in play or in their graveyard during that game (not exile/library) to take as a reward. On a loss, the winning opponent chooses one such card from the loser to remove from their ownership, placing it on the bottom of its card pile.

Rare Feat rewards

If you win a duel and also satisfy any one Rare Feat below, you immediately gain a bonus reward: choose either 1 colorless card or 1 multicolored card.

#Rare Feat condition
1Win with exactly 1 life remaining
2Win with no creatures under your control
3Win with 0 cards in your library
4Win via alternate win condition
5Win by poisoning the opponent

Town Tile procedure (fixed order, final step exclusive)

On a Town Tile, players may perform all actions below in order. The final step is exclusive: choose either Tavern or Questboard.

StepActionRules
1Sanctum (Healing)Remove 1 loss token.
2Market Stall (Trade / Purchase)Trade up to 3 unused cards matching the town’s color; for each traded card gain 1 random new card of the same color and same rarity. Purchase: Common 1 Arcstone, Uncommon 2, Rare 3. Purchase requires at least 3 Arcstones. Reveal 3 cards of the town’s color; buy any/all; unpurchased go to the bottom of their respective piles.
3Manavault (Shop)Buy Links.
4Tavern or QuestboardTavern: spend 1 Arcstone for 1 Rumor, or spend the night for 3 Rumors (skip your next turn). Questboard: each town has exactly 3 quests (including 1 Expedition).

Quest procedure

Questboard is the last town action. Choosing a quest means: next turn you start the chosen quest by placing the corresponding quest tile ahead; the following turn you move onto that tile and resolve it. The quest tile remains on the board after resolution. If you lose, return the quest tile and move back 1 tile; normal loss consequences apply; the quest is considered failed for now.

Endgame: The Dread Nexus and Merrevia Sal

Endgame entry condition

A player enters endgame when they are Level E and have 3 clues of different colors, one of each clue type (Ice Pillar Inscriptions, Halls of Echoes, Singing Crystals).

The Dread Nexus (horizontal double tile)

The Dread Nexus blocks other players’ path; if another player reaches it in time, they must engage it as well.

Dungeon effectDetails
Null SuppressionPlayers can’t cast more than one spell each turn. Dungeon opponents are not affected.

The Eternal Watcher (guardian)

Life: 20. Mythic Slots: 5. Deck color determination uses the player’s Open Weave / Confluence Alignment Codex (Attunement-based) method.

The Voidbringer (mini-boss)

Life: 30. Mythic Slots: 5. Deck color determination uses the player’s Open Weave / Attunement resolver method.

Extra ability: once each duel, as a sorcery, exile target spell on the stack.

Merrevia Sal (final duel)

Merrevia Sal has 99 life. Players duel her and her remaining life persists across fights (does not reset). A player may spare her by reducing her to exactly 1 life; this choice has consequences in later ElderForge editions (post–Ice Age).

Each campaign records whether Merrevia was slain (by player X) or spared (by player Y). Across multiple campaigns, the most common outcome becomes the official resolution that affects future ElderForge expansions.

Notes and remaining open items

Merrevia’s deck assembly and signature abilities are the only major remaining campaign spec to finalize.
