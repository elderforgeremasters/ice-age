ElderForge Campaign

The continent of Terisiare lies frozen. Magic dwindles. Alliances fracture. And beneath the Ohran Ridge, in a seam of stubborn warmth, a remote refuge endures: The Emberhold.

Officially, the Emberhold is neutral ground — a treaty-sanctioned sanctuary where apprentice mages can rest, trade, and train without becoming property of the great color academies. In an Ice Age, a safe hearth is not a luxury; it is a political miracle.

Unofficially, it is something sharper: a hidden research cell studying the world’s strangest threat — Null interference. When the mana flow stutters, anchored spellcraft becomes brittle. The Emberhold teaches Open Weave: stay unanchored, read the day’s flow like weather, and survive instability through adaptation.

Core conventions

Basic lands live outside libraries

Basic lands are not shuffled into duel libraries. Keep basic lands in face-up piles. At any draw step, you may draw a basic land from outside the game instead of drawing from your library.

Zones that matter for campaign rewards

After a duel, only cards that were in play or in a graveyard count as eligible for capture rewards. Cards in exile and libraries are not eligible.

Your owned campaign pool begins with:

Deck construction for duels

Owned pool vs loaner library

Your owned campaign pool is the permanent collection you grow over time. Before each duel, you build a loaner library from the shared piles based on Attunement. Loaner cards are returned after the duel.

Personal slots

You also have personal slots: a small set of non-mythic cards from your owned pool that you keep between duels. Your personal slot count equals your current level (Level E counts as 6). Slots are filled/changed only at Campfires.

Attunement (what it is)

Before each duel, each player performs Attunement: roll 6 custom mana dice with faces . Here signifies Null — a stutter in the Weave where “normal” mana won’t answer cleanly.

Important: Count Nulls immediately. This is your Stability Count and it determines whether you add a Prism Pack or Toolkits. Resolvers can reroll Null dice for color selection, but they do not change your Stability Count.

Stable Attunement vs Null interference

Stability Count (Nulls in the initial roll)Deck add-on
0 Null (stable)Add the Confluence Prism Pack (5 multicolor cards).
1–3 NullAdd that many Relic Toolkits (5 colorless cards each).
4+ NullAdd 3 Relic Toolkits (maximum; extra Null has no further effect).

Colors you play

Use the Attunement Codex to convert your 6-die roll into “Colors you play” (including the resolver rules).

Loaner draw (color cards)

  • Mono or two colors: draw 20 cards from the relevant color piles.
  • Three colors: draw 24 cards from the relevant color piles.
  • Five colors (equal): draw 25 cards (5 from each color).
  • Five colors (primary): draw 28 cards as 8/5/5/5/5 (8 of the primary color, 5 of each other color).

Snowcraft (Snowbank)

Snow-Covered basic lands are kept outside all libraries in five Snowbank piles (one per color).

Snowcraft: For each color you are playing this duel, you begin with Snowcraft 1 for that color. Each time you would draw a card, you may instead take a Snow-Covered basic of a color you are playing from its Snowbank pile. Doing so spends 1 Snowcraft for that color. When a color’s Snowcraft is 0, you cannot take additional Snow-Covered basics of that color this duel.

Rewards can permanently increase your Snowcraft for a specific color (maximum 3).

Non-clue duels

Generic foes (30)

Generic foes are the standard duels of the Journey bag. Each has a preset primary color and a Signature Eternal keyword token. Once each duel, when a creature enters play under that foe’s control, that player may put their Signature Eternal keyword token on that creature.

Generic foe secondary color / toolkit (Method 1): Roll 1 color die. If it matches the foe’s primary color, the foe is mono-color (20 cards). If it shows a different color, the foe is two-color (primary + rolled color, 10 cards each). If it shows Null, the foe gains 1 Relic Toolkit and you roll again for the secondary color; if the second roll is Null, the foe stays mono + toolkit; otherwise add the rolled color as secondary (if it matches primary, stay mono + toolkit).

Relic tile

When you reveal a Relic tile, flip a coin. If you win the flip, gain 1 Mythic card from the Reliquary pile. Otherwise, gain 1 card from the Colorless pile.

Dungeons

Dungeon Entrance tiles are Guardian duels. You must defeat the Guardian on the first try to enter. On a Guardian win, place the corresponding double-size Dungeon tile (Mystery then Boss). Boss duels may be retried unlimited times.

Boss win reward: +3 Ascension and gain 3 random Crystals, then roll Treasure immediately and gain an Ice Pillar Inscription clue of that dungeon’s color.

Town Tile procedure (fixed order, final step exclusive)

On a Town Tile, players may perform all actions below in order. The final step is exclusive: choose either Tavern or Questboard.

StepActionRules
1 Market Stall (Trade / Purchase) Trade: trade up to 3 unused cards matching the town’s color; for each traded card gain 1 random new card of the same color and same rarity.
Purchase: reveal 3 cards of the town’s color; buy any/all; unpurchased go to the bottom of their piles. Costs: Common = 1 Frost, Uncommon = 1 Sky, Rare = 1 Fire.
2Manavault (Shop)Buy Links (see “Links”).
3 Tavern or Questboard Tavern: spend 1 Frost Crystal for 1 Rumor, or spend the night for 3 Rumors (skip your next turn).
Questboard: each town has exactly 3 quests (including 1 Expedition).

Quest procedure

Questboard is the last Town action. Choosing a quest means: next turn you start the chosen quest by placing the corresponding quest tile ahead; the following turn you move onto that tile and resolve it. The quest tile remains on the board after resolution.

If you lose: you gain no rewards; return the quest tile to its bag and move back 1 tile. The quest is considered failed for now.

Duel rewards

Card capture rule

After each win, the winner may choose one card the opponent owns that was in play or in their graveyard during that game (not exile/library) to take as a reward. On a loss, the winning opponent chooses one such card from the loser to remove from their ownership, placing it on the bottom of its card pile.

Rare Feat rewards

If you win a duel and also satisfy any one Rare Feat below, you immediately gain a bonus reward: choose either 1 colorless card or 1 multicolored card.

Endgame: The Dread Nexus and Merrevia Sal

Endgame entry condition

A player enters endgame when they are Level E and have 3 clues of different colors, one of each clue type (Ice Pillar Inscriptions, Halls of Echoes, Singing Crystals).

The Dread Nexus (horizontal double tile)

The Dread Nexus blocks other players’ path; if another player reaches it in time, they must engage it as well.

Dungeon effectDetails
Null SuppressionPlayers can’t cast more than one spell each turn. Dungeon opponents are not affected.

The Eternal Watcher (guardian)

Life: 20. Mythic Slots: 5. Deck color determination uses the player’s Open Weave / Attunement Codex method (Attunement-based).

The Voidbringer (mini-boss)

Life: 30. Mythic Slots: 5. Deck color determination uses the same method.

Extra ability: once each duel, as a sorcery, exile target spell on the stack.

Merrevia Sal (final duel)

Merrevia Sal has 99 life. Players duel her and her remaining life persists across fights (does not reset). A player may spare her by reducing her to exactly 1 life; this choice has consequences in later ElderForge editions (post–Ice Age).

Each campaign records whether Merrevia was slain (by player X) or spared (by player Y). Across multiple campaigns, the most common outcome becomes the official resolution that affects future ElderForge expansions.
