About the Project

ElderForge — Ice Age started as a love letter to early-90s Magic: The Gathering: the mood, the names, the strange little rules, and that specific “my friend owns exactly one copy of this rare and we treat it like a sacred artifact” energy.

The goal is to preserve the nostalgic simplicity of the original Ice Age era while polishing the rough edges: cleaner templating, smoother play patterns, and modern frames — without turning the cards into a wall of text. Power is kept aligned to each card’s original rarity, while still accounting for modern expectations and (power creep — the gradual increase in average card strength over the years).

Visuals are treated as part of the rules: artwork is redesigned to be consistent, natural, and lore-grounded, matching each card’s character, mechanics, and tone — no uncanny CGI vibes, no “this looks like a different universe every card” whiplash.

Lore-first, not just flavor text

The project is also a deep dive into the original Ice Age lore — especially Terisiare — carefully preserved and expanded for maximum immersion. Every card has its own flavor text, and the wider campaign format ties gameplay rewards and progression directly into the world’s people, places, and conflicts.

On top of the remastered Ice Age block, the project includes 168 additional mythic rare cards designed to close lore gaps: legendary characters, events, and locations that were mentioned but never properly featured in the original printings. In the ElderForge campaign, these mythics appear as achievement rewards and progression milestones — the “story treasure,” not just raw power.

Everything here is fan-made for a private playgroup and exists to celebrate the craft: tighter design, clearer rules, and a world that feels like it actually has weather.
