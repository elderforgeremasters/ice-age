About the Project

ElderForge — Ice Age started as a love letter to early‑90s Magic: The Gathering — the mood, the names, the strange little rules, and that specific “my friend owns exactly one copy of this rare and we treat it like a sacred artifact” energy.

The mission is restoration, not reinvention: keep the nostalgic clarity of the originals, but sand down the pain points that time exposed. Templating is streamlined, frames are modern, and the play patterns are tuned so the cards feel crisp without turning into a wall of keywords. Power stays faithful to each card’s original rarity while accounting for modern baselines (power creep — the gradual increase in average card strength over the years).

The art is treated like rules text with a soul: every piece is built to match the card’s character, mechanics, and tone — consistent direction, natural materials, grounded anatomy, and Ice Age atmosphere. No CGI sheen, no random style whiplash. Each illustration is meant to feel like it always belonged on Terisiare… just finally rendered with the care the setting deserved.

Lore-first, not just flavor text

Ice Age isn’t just “snow set.” It’s nations under pressure, old grudges calcifying, magic thinning out, and a continent (Terisiare) that feels big enough to get lost in. ElderForge dives into that lore and treats it as a foundation: names, places, cultures, and the quiet connective tissue that makes a card feel like a real artifact from a real world.

Every card carries its own voice, but the project goes further: 168 additional mythic rare cards were designed to tie off loose ends the original printings only hinted at — legendary figures, pivotal events, and locations that deserved to be more than a line of flavor text. In the ElderForge campaign, these mythics aren’t “pack luck.” They’re earned as rewards for progression and achievements — story‑anchored power, delivered at the moments it actually means something.

This is fan‑made, private‑playgroup craft work: obsessive, lore‑faithful, and built to be played — the kind of project that exists because the world was worth preserving properly.
